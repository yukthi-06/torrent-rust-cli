# User Prompt
> some torrents stuck at 99.9%

# Implementation
- Identified that torrents were getting stuck near completion due to dead or stalled peers silently holding onto the final remaining pieces.
- Because the `PeerMessage::read(stream).await?` operation was an unbounded, blocking read, a peer that abruptly stopped sending data (e.g., choked us without sending the `Choke` message, or its TCP connection went half-open/silent) would cause the daemon's read loop to hang indefinitely.
- While the read loop hung, the `PieceGuard` held the final piece hostage. Since the piece was never refunded to `missing_pieces`, no other peers could step in to finish the download, leaving the torrent frozen at 99.9%.
- Wrapped `PeerMessage::read` with a 15-second `tokio::time::timeout`. If a peer stalls for 15 seconds, the timeout triggers, the read loop breaks, the connection is dropped, and the `PieceGuard` instantly requeues the piece for an active peer to complete.
- Applied the same timeout logic to `Handshake::read` (10 seconds) to prevent frozen connections during the initial handshake.
